About Us

Constitutional Education
at the Grassroots

Who We Are

Katiba Na Mia Mia is a civic education movement led by Ng'ang'a Muigai. We simplify Kenya's Constitution for citizens in both Kiswahili and English, committed to taking constitutional knowledge directly to the people at the grassroots level.

What We Do

We conduct civic education and public engagement on constitutional matters across Kenya. Our mission is to make the Constitution accessible and empower citizens with knowledge of their rights and responsibilities through community engagement.

When We Act

As a 47 weeks project, we conduct civic education on a weekly basis in every county from Wednesdays to Fridays. One county, one week at a time, until every Kenyan has access to constitutional knowledge.

How We Engage

We engage and teach through interactive debates, katiba literacy tests, and use music performances for mobilization. Our approach makes constitutional education dynamic, memorable, and accessible to all.

Why We Exist

We are pushing for transformative changes through the KAMAU AGENDA: making the Constitution a mandatory subject in schools, establishing education infrastructure standards, creating a chronic diseases fund, reforming elections, and standardizing devolution.

Our Impact

Through petitions, policy drafting, bill creation, and grassroots education, we're liberating future generations from the shackles of ignorance. Knowledge is power, and constitutional literacy is the foundation of democratic participation.

The KAMAU Agenda

Why We Fight:
Five Pillars for Change

We are seeking transformative constitutional and policy changes that will reshape Kenya's future. The KAMAU Agenda represents our commitment to systemic reform across five critical areas.

K

Katiba (Constitution)

Pushing for the Constitution of Kenya to be established as a full mandatory and examinable subject in the basic education curriculum. Constitutional literacy must start in the classroom.

A

Masomo (Education)

Establishment of basic infrastructure standards for classrooms, administration blocks, libraries, dormitories, and teachers quarters in every public school in Kenya. Quality education requires quality infrastructure.

M

Afya (Health)

Establishment of a chronic diseases fund separate from the health budget. Kenyans battling long-term illnesses deserve dedicated, sustainable support beyond general healthcare funding.

A

Uchaguzi (Elections)

Limit the recall process of members of parliament to the High Court under the Elections Act. Ensuring accountability while maintaining judicial oversight and democratic integrity.

U

Ugatuzi (Devolution)

Establish a devolution prototype to serve as the basic development standard for every county. Equal opportunities and resources for all Kenyans, regardless of geographic location.
